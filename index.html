STAGE 05

Flagging isn't
the same as understanding.

Detection tells you what changed. AI tells you what it means — drawing on codes, calculation methods, and historical Lummus projects. Not a chatbot. Embedded engineering judgement that reads the graph, interprets it, and recommends the right next move.

```
⚠️ Validation · Stress Analysis
Reduced wall thickness increases tube stress by approximately 18%, bringing projected stress to 21.2 ksi — above the allowable limit of 20 ksi at the current design temperature of 1650°F.
📚 Historical Reference · 3 similar projects
SRT IV (Shell Pernis, 2021) and SRT V (ExxonMobil Baytown, 2023) both hit this same tradeoff. In each case, engineering teams resolved with reduced support spacing rather than upgraded tube material — avoiding ~$34k per heater in material costs.
💡 Recommended path forward
  • Option A — Reduce support spacing from 6.0 ft to 4.5 ft. Keeps current material, brings stress within allowable. Lowest cost.
  • Option B — Upgrade tube material to A213 T91. Higher allowable stress at temperature. Keeps current geometry.
```
